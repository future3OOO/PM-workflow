## 7b) Maintenance — owner approval request (Tapi — inspection follow-up)

> Hi [Owner first name],
>
> These items were noted during the routine inspection at [address]. Are you happy for me to arrange a contractor to attend to them?
>
> Kind regards,
> [PM / VA name]

**When to use:** After a routine inspection, for each maintenance item synced to Tapi where the owner has not already responded via the inspection report email.

**When NOT to use:** If the owner has already replied to the inspection report email approving the work — go directly to sending a work order instead.

# WEBSITE COPY — Fixed‑Term Lease Break Guide
A fixed-term tenancy agreement is legally binding for the full agreed term. However, if your circumstances change, you can request an early termination — and we’ll do our best to work with you.

Breaking a lease isn’t the same as giving notice, so there’s a specific process you’ll need to follow. This guide walks you through everything step by step.

## 1) Submit your lease break request (in writing)
If you’d like to end your tenancy early, the first step is letting us know — formally and in writing. Your request must:
- Be submitted in writing
- Be sent via email to your Property Manager
- Clearly state your requested vacate date

**Important:** A lease break is not automatic. It requires landlord approval before anything moves forward. Please don’t make moving arrangements until you’ve received written confirmation.

**Your vacate date is a commitment.** Once you propose a vacate date and we relist the property based on that date, you are committed to it. If we secure a new tenant to move in from your proposed date, you must vacate by that date — even if your plans change and it no longer suits you. Please make sure you’re confident in the date before submitting your request.

## 2) Lease break fee (typically 1 week rent — check your agreement)
If your lease break is approved, a fee will apply. Most of our tenancy agreements specify a flat fee equal to one week’s rent — but you should always check your individual agreement to confirm the exact amount for your property.

The fee is:
- Payable in full upon approval
- Non-refundable
- Put toward advertising and administration costs

What the fee covers:
- Advertising / Trade Me listing fees
- Processing applications, reference and credit checks
- Preparing new tenancy documentation
- Coordinating inspections and compliance checks
- Agent time (viewings and screening)

**Reality check:** The actual advertising cost alone is usually around ~$300. When you add the agent’s time to find a replacement, we are often breaking even or losing money on this fee. Other firms will often charge on a per-viewing basis or an hourly rate. We charge a simple flat fee strictly to cover costs, not to profit.

## 3) Ongoing rent obligations
You are required to continue paying rent until:
- A suitable replacement tenant starts a new tenancy, or
- The original fixed-term end date is reached — whichever occurs first.

**Important:** You remain responsible for rent even if:
- You have already moved out
- The property is sitting vacant
- You have returned the keys

Rent is payable right up to the day a new tenant legally takes over the property. There is no guarantee a replacement tenant will be secured immediately — typically it takes around one month from listing a property to a new tenant moving in. You should always budget for an overlap of at least one to two weeks.

We don’t just accept the first application that comes in. Any replacement tenant must pass the same reference and credit checks that you did — they need to be a reasonable fit for the property.

## 4) Relisting & viewings
Once your lease break is approved, we get to work straight away:
- The property will be relisted for rent immediately
- Viewings will be scheduled as soon as possible
- Marketing will begin without delay

Want to reduce your overlapping costs? Make the property as available and presentable as possible. The easier it is for us to show and the better it looks, the faster we can secure a replacement tenant — and the sooner your rent obligations end.

## 5) Vacant possession requirement
Once a replacement tenant has been approved, you’ll need to provide **2–3 clear days** of vacant possession before the new tenancy begins. This gives us time to complete:
- Your exit inspection
- Cleaning checks
- Property condition review
- Entry inspection for the new tenant

We’ll coordinate dates with you so the handover is as smooth as possible.
